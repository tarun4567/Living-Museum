About the Asiatic Lion

The Asiatic lion (Panthera leo persica) is native to the Gir Forest in Gujarat, India. They are smaller than African lions, with distinct belly folds and shorter manes. Once widespread across Asia, their range has now shrunk drastically. Conservation efforts have helped increase their population, but they remain critically endangered.

Threats & Conservation

Asiatic lions face threats from habitat loss, poaching, disease, and human conflict. Conservation includes:

  • Protecting Gir Forest & expanding safe habitats
  • Anti-poaching patrols
  • Community education & eco-tourism
  • Creating a second lion population to prevent extinction
