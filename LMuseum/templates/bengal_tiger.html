About the Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is a subspecies of tiger native to the Indian subcontinent. It is the national animal of India and is one of the most iconic and majestic creatures in the wild. Found in forests, mangroves, and grasslands, these tigers are symbols of strength and power.

Physical Appearance

1. Coat: Bright orange with dark black stripes.
2. Stripes: Unique to each tiger.
3. Size: Ranges from 7 to 12 feet long.
4. Tail: Long and ringed, used for balance.
5. Eyes & Ears: Sharp senses with white ear markings.
6. Paws: Retractable claws for stealth.
7. Weight: Between 220 to 660 pounds.

Why Are They Endangered?

1. Habitat Loss: Due to deforestation and urban expansion.
2. Poaching: Killed for skin, bones, and trade.
3. Conflict: Killed in retaliation by locals.
4. Prey Loss: Decline in deer and wild boars.
5. Climate Change: Affects forest and mangrove ecosystems.
