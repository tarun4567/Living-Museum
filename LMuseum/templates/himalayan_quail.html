Himalayan Quail

Extinct Animals of India

About the Himalayan Quail

The Himalayan quail (Ophrysia superciliosa) was a bird species found in the Western Himalayas, especially in India and Pakistan. Known for its unique appearance and shy behavior, it preferred dense undergrowth and was last reliably seen in the 1870s. Despite surveys, it is now believed to be extinct, with habitat destruction and hunting being major causes.

Physical Appearance

1. Appearance: Males had a black crown, face, and throat, with a white eyebrow line. The body was brown-gray with fine bars.
2. Habitat: Thrived in oak and rhododendron forests at high elevations.
3. Behavior: Shy, ground-dwelling, and rarely seen.
4. Status: Discovered in the 19th century, it was already rare and hasn't been seen since the 1870s.

Reasons for Extinction

1. Habitat Loss: Deforestation and development in the Himalayas.
2. Hunting: Heavily hunted during colonial rule for sport.
3. Introduced Species: Dogs, cats, and other predators threatened eggs and chicks.
4. Small Population: Naturally rare, making it highly vulnerable.
5. Limited Range: Only found in a small area, reducing its resilience.
6. Climate Change: Environmental shifts likely affected habitat and food sources.
