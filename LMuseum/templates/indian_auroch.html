Indian Auroch

Extinct Animal of India

About the Indian Auroch

The Indian auroch, also known as Bos primigenius namadicus, was a powerful wild cattle species native to the Indian subcontinent. Considered the ancestor of todayâ€™s domesticated zebu cattle, it sadly became extinct in the early 20th century due to human impact.

The Physical Appearance of the Indian Auroch

1. Size: 5 to 6.5 feet tall at the shoulder, weighing up to 1,500 kg.
2. Build: Robust, muscular with deep chest and strong limbs.
3. Horns: Long, curved horns spreading outward and slightly up.
4. Coat: Dark brown to black; shaggy in winters.
5. Shoulder Hump: Prominent and sturdy.
6. White Rump: Distinctive patch visible on the rear.

Reasons for the Extinction of the Indian Auroch

1. Habitat Loss: Land converted for farming and urban use.
2. Overhunting: Hunted for meat, hide, and horns.
3. Competition: Outcompeted by domestic cattle.
4. Disease: Caught infections from domesticated species.
5. Genetic Isolation: Small populations led to weak genetic diversity.
