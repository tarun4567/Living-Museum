Indian Elephant

Endangered Wildlife of India

About the Indian Elephant

The Indian elephant is a subspecies of the Asian elephant, native to the Indian subcontinent. Revered in Indian culture, it plays a key role in religion, festivals, and ecology. However, due to habitat destruction, human conflict, and poaching, they face significant threats.

Physical Appearance

1. Size: Stands 10‚Äì13 feet tall; males are generally larger.
2. Body: Robust with a single back hump (unlike the African elephant).
3. Ears: Smaller, shaped somewhat like India.
4. Trunk: Muscular, versatile, and prehensile.
5. Tusks: Typically seen in males; curved ivory tusks are at high risk of poaching.

Why Are They Endangered?

1. Habitat Loss: Deforestation and expansion reduce their space.
2. Human Conflict: Crop raiding leads to retaliation.
3. Ivory Trade: Illegal tusk hunting persists.
4. Fragmented Populations: Isolated herds reduce gene diversity.
5. Climate Change: Alters their access to water and food.
