Indian Rhinoceros

Endangered Animal of India

About the Indian Rhinoceros

The Indian rhinoceros is native to the Indian subcontinent, primarily found in parts of India and Nepal. It is the largest of the rhinoceros species and can weigh up to 2,300 kilograms. Known for their single horn, Indian rhinos are herbivores typically living in grasslands and wetlands.

The Physical Appearance of Indian Rhinoceros

1. Size: They stand about 1.4 to 1.85 meters tall and can reach lengths up to 3.7 meters.
2. Skin: Gray to dark gray, thick with folds, giving them an armored look.
3. Horn: A single horn made of keratin, often exceeding 50 cm.
4. Folded Skin: Plate-like folds resemble natural armor.
5. Body Build: Bulky with a neck hump and short tail.
6. Face: Flat with small eyes and ears.
7. Legs: Sturdy with three-toed hooves.

Reasons for Their Endangerment

1. Habitat Loss: Due to agriculture, deforestation, and encroachment.
2. Poaching: For their horns, used in traditional medicine and as status symbols.
3. Hunting: Especially during colonial times.
4. Human-Wildlife Conflict: Retaliation due to crop damage or aggression.
5. Natural Disasters: Flooding and other environmental shifts displace them from habitats.
