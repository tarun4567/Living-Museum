Malabar Civet

Extinct or Critically Endangered Animals of India

About the Malabar Civet

The Malabar civet (Viverra civettina) is a nocturnal mammal native to the Western Ghats of India. It's one of the rarest mammals in the world, known for its slender body, dark brown to black fur, and distinctive white facial markings. This elusive creature is critically endangered and possibly extinct due to massive habitat destruction and illegal poaching.

Reasons for Extinction or Decline

1. Habitat Loss: Deforestation and agricultural expansion in the Western Ghats.
2. Poaching: Hunted for fur and body parts, leading to a severe population drop.
3. Habitat Fragmentation: Isolated forest patches disrupt breeding and survival.
4. Lack of Awareness: Limited conservation efforts and public knowledge.
