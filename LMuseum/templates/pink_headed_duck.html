About the Pink Headed Duck

The pink-headed duck (Rhodonessa caryophyllacea) is a rare and possibly extinct waterfowl species once found in India, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. Known for its beautiful pink or reddish-pink head, it has not been conclusively sighted in decades, but still holds hope among conservationists.

Physical Appearance

1. Pink Head: Bright pink or reddish-pink head that stands out.
2. Body: Sleek brown to dark gray body adapted for wetland life.
3. Bill: Broad, flat bluish-black bill.
4. Eyes: Dark eyes often surrounded by a subtle white ring.
5. Size: About 45â€“55 cm in length.
6. Sexual Dimorphism: Males and females look very similar.

Reasons for Decline

1. Habitat Loss: Conversion of wetlands into farmland and cities.
2. Hunting: Hunted for meat and ornamental purposes.
3. Wetland Degradation: Pollution and loss of nesting grounds.
4. Limited Range: Small and fragmented populations made it more vulnerable.
